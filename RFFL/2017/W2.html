If you took a poll around the league, I'd bet a majority of managers would be surprised at their Week 2 rankings.
The resulting change from a loss or roster change can be really emphasized until about W5.
I always try to curtail my initial Fantasy Football depression until about that point. If you are like me though, the season as never not started rough.
That leads me to today, where I'm not sitting where I want to. I'm sure the others below me aren't happy either.
Take some time, breath and understand, there is always next week. And the mighty can fall just as easy as you can, too.

Also, if you get a chance, look at my soapbox below the rankings. I enjoyed researching and writing it, I hope you enjoy reading it.



The Deflateriot's Soapbox

Divisional Domination!

Since the expansion to 12-teams, the RFFL has been given an odd problem. I’ll shed some light on it by offering you some questions to ask yourself.

  • Do you ever feel like your division never succeeds?
  • Do any number of wins in a streak make you feel like playoffs are guaranteed to you?
  • Are you surprised when you get a win against someone outside your division?
If you answered “Yes” to any or all the above questions, you might be in RFFL – EAST.
Let me explain, slowly….



Above are three tables.
Fig. 1 – Overall Wins for the past 2 Regular seasons.
Fig. 2 – Overall Point Differential for the past 2 regular seasons.
Fig. 3 – Overall Wins, Ranked by Point Differential for the past 2 regular seasons.

Anyone surprised by the position of where other managers fell? Surprised by your own position?

If you are DOMS or above in any of those figures, congratulations. You have 50%+ win record! If you’d like your hard stats, hit me up, I like rubbing those things into people’s faces. If those figures make you sad, don’t give up, it might get worse.



Time to shed more light on the Divisional Domination, with two more figures above.
Fig. 4 – Divisional Breakdown of RFFL
Fig. 5 – Divisional Breakdown of RFFL with (Regular Season) Win/Loss for past 2 years

If there is anything to take away from Figure 5, it should stand out that not one manager in RFFL – EAST has a winning record over the past 2 seasons combined. Even more astonishing, no manager has been placed higher than 6th in the end of season rankings in that time.

Now the top of the iceberg.
In that same period of 2 years, NORTH and WEST have had 3 Top 5 appearances, and South has had 4 Top 5 appearances. To further grasp the domination over EAST, NORTH and SOUTH both have 1 Championship appearance (with NORTH winning 1), and WEST has 2 Championship appearances (with 1 win). I didn’t mention EAST, did I?

“Well Deflateriots, EAST might be the toughest division to come out of, so all your statistics lie about how soft they appear.” WRONG! I present another figure EAST isn’t going to like.



Fig. 6 – Divisional Breakdown w/ Reg Season Win/Loss, Point Diff over past 2 seasons

See any gleaming unbalanced numbers staring back at you?
The amount of point differential that EAST has as a division is telling of the production out of those teams. The weird part about bringing up their production is, as a division EAST has put up the second most points (2302) when facing teams in their own division (First is WEST, putting up 2407 against their own, as a division). Everything falls apart once they go off to fight others.

The other number that stands out is SOUTH’s +381 differential. KK has been the most dominate team in the regular season since the expansion. (No championship wins to show for it though. HA!)



Fig. 7 – Div. Breakdown w/ Reg Season W/L, Point Diff and BYE Week over past 2 Seasons

Yea, along with all the weight bearing down on EAST, they haven’t pulled off a BYE week, given the equal chance to.

Now why is this all relevant?
Maybe I’m trying to show EAST that they have a chance to rise above it all, and be the Disney story of RFFL? (As of Week 2, they lead in Point Differential with +22, followed by NORTH with +2)
I could possibly find the lopsidedness of divisional strength to be unfair to the little man.
This could be my soapbox asking for a shake up next season, of both the schedules and/or divisions.

Whatever you take away from this, I hope you notice how lucky you are if you live in NORTH, SOUTH or WEST. Our domination over EAST is well enough that I had to write about it.
