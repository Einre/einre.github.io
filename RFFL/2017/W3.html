Trust: An Excercise in Blind Faith

I like starting off by making you all answer a question. This is how a lot of the articles are going to go.

  • Without looking at the players section of ESPN, could you confidently name the NFL teams most rostered by RFFL through 3 weeks?
  • And furthermore, could guess the cumulative number of roster spots they have accrued through 3 weeks of play?
Everyone playing fantasy football has some knowledge of the stuff. Even if its “Higher projected players get started”, this isn’t golf. (Some managers seem to be playing that way though.)

This week I thought I’d take an early exploration through some numbers and percentages of NFL teams that seem to spew confidence for RFFL, and those that are lying to us.



Figure 1 is showing us the number of accumulative roster spots each NFL has been rostered through 3 weeks of fantasy football by the RFFL. Does anything surprise you?

Some thoughts:
  • The top dog, NE, shouldn’t surprise anyone.
  • The bottom dog, SF, feels like it is where it should be.
  • For KC metro managers, the Chiefs look under-represented.
  • It is a bit early to make definitive conclusions, but this list has some curious placements.


Figure 2 shows us the amount of points each rostered NFL team has accumulated for RFFL managers across 3 weeks of play.

Some thoughts here:
  • NE shouldn’t surprise anyone with their domination.
  • The bottom 5 don’t feel like they have any players worthy of a roster spot.
  • KC is second in points scored. Hmmmm?
  • Through 3 weeks, we have got 4411 pts out of our rosters. (Starters & Benches combined)


Figure 3 shows us the amount each rostered NFL team is averaging per player.

Some thoughts:
  • The overall average is 9.64 pts/player.
  • KC rostered players are averaging almost twice the Rostered NFL average.
  • BAL and NYJ are playing at below half the Rostered NFL average.
  • Any team in the smooth gradient (14.60 – 6.94) is looking normal. Those outliers will come around, hopefully.


Figure 4 gives us the overall view of all the numerical values of Rostered NFL teams.

This is the table that can either confirm or deny your complaints about certain teams underperforming, or overperforming in relation to the number of roster spots they have in our league.

Points of interest:
  • BAL is the 7th most rostered team in our league, though they are the 8th least producing team for RFFL. Combining for a measly average of 4.53 pts/player, that is under half the average. A lot of blind faith from RFFL managers towards the Ravens.
  • KC is 17th most rostered, 2nd in point totals, and 1st in average pts/player. Either RFFL managers are efficient when it comes to “hometown”, or KC is an outlier. I’d like to say we as managers are efficient, but I think so far, some performances on that team have been outliers. Prove me wrong, KC.
  • SF is the 32nd most rostered, 28th in point totals, and 2 in average pts/player. This is a case of blind votes of “no confidence.” A team performing 5 pt/player above average needs to be given a second chance. I know fantasy-wise SF doesn’t scream “WIN!”, but the trend right leads me to believe that team has something, I just don’t know what that something is.
This is a topic I’ll certainly revisit. It will be interesting if the sway of RFFL confidence in the teams that are outperforming or being lackluster will change how this looks. For now it looks like those who rostered NE, GB, MIN, PHI or KC are probably winning games and feeling good about it.
